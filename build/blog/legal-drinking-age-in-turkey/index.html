Legal Drinking Age in Turkey

Turkey legal drinking age

In Turkey, if you've celebrated your 18th birthday, you're legally allowed to both buy and consume alcohol. This rule is firmly in place throughout the country, irrespective of whether you find yourself in a bustling city such as Istanbul or a quaint, secluded village.

Drinking age in Turkey: Not anyone drinks in Turkey

While the law permits alcohol consumption for anyone aged 18 or more, Turkey, home to a predominantly Muslim population, often sees cultural norms impacting people's attitude towards drinking. While Turkey is constitutionally secular, there might be conservative individuals or groups that don't appreciate alcohol consumption, particularly during religious periods such as Ramadan.

Remember, public intoxication and drunk driving are strictly off-limits in Turkey and can lead to serious repercussions, including hefty fines, jail time, or even deportation if you're a foreigner. As always, be a responsible drinker, and ensure you've arranged a safe journey back home.

In Turkey, your local store can sell alcohol between the hours of 10 am and 10 pm. Also, no alcohol sales are allowed within 100 meters of educational institutions and religious buildings. Post these timings, your only option for a drink would be bars, restaurants, or similar establishments.

The Turkish government keeps a tight lid on alcohol advertising, so don't be surprised if you don't spot any billboards or TV ads promoting booze.

Turkey drinking culture

Despite the regulations, you'll find that alcohol holds a place of significance in Turkish society, especially in the urban centers. Turks have a glorious history of winemaking, and their national spirit, raki – a unique anise-flavored drink – is a popular choice at many gatherings. Experiencing the warmth of a 'meyhane', or a Turkish tavern, is a must-do, but only if you're legally old enough to drink.

Wrapping Up

In a nutshell, as long as you're 18 or older, you're allowed to drink in Turkey. However, remember to always be aware of your surroundings and adhere to the local traditions and societal expectations. Soak in the vibrant spirit of Turkey and whether you're raising a toast with raki or unwinding with a beer along the Bosphorus, do it responsibly.

And to that, we say "Şerefe!" or Cheers!
